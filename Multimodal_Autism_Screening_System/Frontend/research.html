Multimodal Deep Learning Framework for Autism Spectrum Disorder Screening

Authors: Shreshth Gupt
Date: November 2025
Status: Under Evaluation

This paper presents a comprehensive multimodal approach to ASD screening by integrating behavioral questionnaires, acoustic features from speech, and facial image analysis. Our fusion network achieves 93% accuracy, outperforming state-of-the-art single-modality approaches.

Related Work & References

Acoustic-Prosodic Features for Autism Detection

Authors: Bone et al.
Year: 2015
Accuracy: 84%

Pioneering work on using prosodic features (pitch, intensity) from speech for ASD detection using SVM classifiers.

Deep CNN for Facial Expression in ASD

Authors: Hasan et al.
Year: 2018
Accuracy: 85%

Applied deep convolutional neural networks for facial expression recognition in children with autism using transfer learning.

Multimodal Fusion for Autism Detection

Authors: El-Gendy et al.
Year: 2020
Accuracy: 91%

Combined behavioral and audio features using early fusion strategies with neural networks, achieving 91% accuracy.

🔬 Dataset

This research uses the Kaggle Multimodal Autism Dataset:

  • Source: Kaggle Dataset Link
  • Modalities: Behavioral (CSV), Acoustic (WAV), Visual (JPG)
  • Samples: 100 subjects (balanced ASD/Non-ASD)
  • Features: 20 behavioral, 40 MFCC coefficients, 224×224 facial images
